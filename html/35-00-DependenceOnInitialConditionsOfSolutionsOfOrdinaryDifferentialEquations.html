dependence on initial conditions of solutions of ordinary differential equations


Let E⊂W where W is a normed vector space, f∈C1⁢(E) is a continuous differentiable map f:E→W. Furthermore consider the ordinary differential equation

x˙=f⁢(x)

with the initial condition

x⁢(0)=x0.

Let x⁢(t) be the solution of the above initial value problem defined as

x:I→E

where I=[-a,a]. Then there exist δ>0 such that for all y0∈Nδ⁢(x0)(y0 in the δ neighborhood of x0) has a unique solution y⁢(t) to the initial value problem above except for the initial value changed to x⁢(0)=y0. In addition y⁢(t) is twice continouously differentialble function of t over the interval I.
